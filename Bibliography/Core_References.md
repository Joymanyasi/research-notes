# 🔍 Core References & Curiosity Points

These 10 papers represent the intellectual anchors of this investigation. For each, I am exploring a "Curiosity Point"—a specific question or tension that connects the biological data to the social reality.

## 🧬 Pillar 1: Genomic Necessity

### 1. [Saleh et al. (2021) - Comparative analysis of TNBC transcriptomics of Kenyan, African American and Caucasian Women](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tranon.2021.101086)
**Curiosity Point:** This study looks specifically at **Kenyan women**. It provides the biological evidence for my project. I am curious how we can turn this "local" biological evidence into a "global" regulatory requirement for AI.

### 2. [Rao et al. (2025) - Mutational landscape of triple-negative breast cancer in African American women](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41588-024-01897-x)
**Curiosity Point:** This paper documents specific mutational patterns in African-ancestry TNBC. My question: If these patterns are the "ground truth" for African women, why are they still considered "atypical" or "noise" in global AI pipelines?

### 3. [Sirugo et al. (2019) - The Missing Diversity in Human Genetic Studies](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2019.02.048)
**Curiosity Point:** This paper quantifies the massive bias in global genomic datasets. I am curious: how do AI models built on this "missing diversity" physically manifest as misdiagnosis in an East African oncology ward?

## 🏛️ Pillar 2: Social Science & Data Justice

### 4. [Birhane (2020) - Algorithmic Colonization of Africa](https://journals.law.ed.ac.uk/script-ed/article/view/1211)
**Curiosity Point:** Birhane argues that "data is the new oil" is an extractive metaphor. I am exploring how this extraction plays out in the genomic "raw material" of East African clinics. Is genomic data the new frontier for colonial extraction?

### 5. [Obermeyer et al. (2019) - Dissecting racial bias in an algorithm used to manage the health of populations](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aax2342)
**Curiosity Point:** This study showed that an algorithm used "cost" as a proxy for "health," accidentally discriminating against Black patients. I am looking for the "hidden proxies" in cancer AI—what are we measuring that we *think* is neutral but isn't?

### 6. [Odero et al. (2024) - The Ubuntu Way: Ensuring Ethical AI Integration in Health Research](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pdig.0000452)
**Curiosity Point:** Can "communal accountability" (Ubuntu) replace "individual consent" as the primary protection for genomic data? I am exploring how this shifts the power from the developer back to the community.

### 7. [Taylor (2017) - What is data justice? The case for connecting digital rights and freedoms globally](https://doi.org/10.1177/2053951717736335)
**Curiosity Point:** Taylor links "visibility" to "justice." I am asking: What does it mean for an East African woman to be "digitally visible" in an AI system without being "biologically exploited"?

### 8. [Agre (1997) - Toward a Critical Technical Practice](https://doi.org/10.1016/B978-012119745-2/50005-2)
**Curiosity Point:** Agre argues that we must be both "inside" and "outside" the technology. This justifies my immersion in GATK pipelines. I am curious: how does "knowing the code" change the way I "write the policy"?

## 📜 Pillar 3: Policy & Governance

### 9. [Gao et al. (2023) - Addressing the Challenge of Biomedical Data Inequality](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-biodatasci-020722-100732)
**Curiosity Point:** This provides the "Medical AI perspective" on inequality. I am analyzing the gap between the technical fixes proposed here and the structural changes needed in African data governance.

### 10. [Kenya National AI Strategy 2025-2030](https://ict.go.ke/)
**Curiosity Point:** This is the local policy landscape. I am investigating the "Regulatory Silence"what specifically is missing from this national strategy regarding genomic oncology AI?
